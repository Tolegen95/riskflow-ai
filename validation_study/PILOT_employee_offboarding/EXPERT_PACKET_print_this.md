## Rating Rubric (keep this page open while scoring)

### Probability (P) — how likely is the threat event?
- **1 (Low):** Threat event unlikely within the review period; no comparable occurrence in the past 12 months and no persistent enabling condition.
- **2 (Medium):** Threat event plausible; occurred elsewhere in the organization/sector within the past 12 months, or enabling conditions are present intermittently.
- **3 (High):** Threat event likely or recurring; has occurred in this process before, or enabling conditions are persistently present.

### Vulnerability (V) — how exploitable is the weakness?
- **1 (Low):** No known exploitable weakness on the path to the threat event; existing safeguards already address it.
- **2 (Medium):** A partial or inconsistently applied weakness exists on the path to the threat event.
- **3 (High):** A known, currently unaddressed weakness exists directly on the path to the threat event.

### Impact (I) — how severe is the consequence if it happens?
- **1 (Low):** Limited, recoverable consequence; resolved within routine operations.
- **2 (Medium):** Moderate consequence requiring management attention.
- **3 (High):** Severe consequence (safety, large-scale exposure, extended critical-system outage, or regulatory exposure).

### Control Effectiveness (CE) — how well is it currently controlled?
- **0.00:** No control in place.
- **0.25:** Control exists but is manual, inconsistently applied, or unverified.
- **0.50:** Control is implemented and applied, but not independently tested or audited.
- **0.75:** Control is implemented, tested, and audited, with minor residual gaps.
- **1.00:** Control is implemented, tested, audited, and has demonstrated effectiveness.
